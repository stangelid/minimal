    My husband and I booked 3 nights at the Hotel Alimandi Vaticano based on the great reviews on Tripadvisor. We were delighted with pretty much everything about our stay there. The room we stayed in was exactly as described on the hotel's website. Elegant decor, nice cool marble floor, very big bed with a firm mattress, reasonably priced mini bar and working air conditioning. The bathroom wasn't huge but it was modern, well equipped and spotlessly clean. We were suprised by how quiet and peaceful the hotel was, considering it's right in the centre of Rome and on quite a busy street. But we didn't hear very much street noise or traffic at all from our room and it was very easy to sleep at night. Breakfast was served downstairs in the dining room. It's self service (buffet) apart from coffee, which is waitress service. The coffee wasn't the greatest - a bit weak and watery - but the food was nice enough, nothing fancy just simple but very well cooked basic things like eggs, sauted potatoes, sausages and grilled vegetables, etc. They also had fresh pastries and croissants, which were very nice too. The rooms don't have ironing facilities but you can use an iron and ironing board belonging to the hotel, if you go down to the basement with your things. We did this on the first evening and while it wasn't ideal, it wasn't a big problem either. As the hotel's right in the middle of town, practically next door to the Vatican, it's perfectly located for sightseeing.
